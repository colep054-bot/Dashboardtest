How is this score determined? ▼
EM Gap 40%

The difference in Adjusted Efficiency Margin between the two teams. A larger gap means one team is clearly stronger. A gap of 30+ pts = max confidence; under 5 pts = near-coinflip.

score = min(|EM_A − EM_B| / 30, 1)
Win Prob Edge 35%

How far the win probability is from a 50/50 split. A 90% win prob is a much stronger prediction than 55%. Derived from the Pythagorean expectation formula with home court applied.

score = |winPct − 50| / 50
Tempo Match 15%

When teams have very different tempos, pace-of-play becomes a wildcard. A slow team dragging a fast team to a grind adds real unpredictability. Smaller tempo gaps = cleaner projection.

score = max(0, 1 − |ΔTempo| / 15)
Location 10%

Home/away games have a known +3.5 pt advantage baked in. Neutral site games score slightly lower since there is no crowd factor to anchor the projection.

home/away = 1.0  |  neutral = 0.75
Final score = (EM Gap × 0.40) + (Win Edge × 0.35) + (Tempo × 0.15) + (Location × 0.10), scaled 0–100 and clamped between 18% (no prediction is certain to fail) and 97% (no prediction is perfectly certain).

How Predictions Are Calculated

▼
01
Adjusted Efficiency Margin
Each team has an AdjEM — the difference between their offensive and defensive efficiency, adjusted for opponent strength. A higher AdjEM = better team overall.
AdjEM = AdjOE − AdjDE
02
Score Projection
Projected points are calculated by combining each team's offense vs the opponent's defense, scaled to the expected number of possessions (tempo).
Score A = (AdjOE_A × AdjDE_B / 10000) × Possessions
03
Home Court Advantage
Home teams receive a +3.5 point adjustment to their projected score. Neutral site games receive no adjustment. Away games flip the advantage.
Home: +1.75 pts each side
Away: −1.75 pts each side
04
Win Probability
Win probability uses a Pythagorean expectation formula (exponent 11.5) — the same model KenPom uses — then adjusts ±4% for home court.
P(win) = OE^11.5 / (OE^11.5 + DE^11.5)
Home adj: ±4%
05
Pace Model
Possessions drive scoring. The pace model defaults to the average tempo of both teams, but you can force the slower or faster team to dictate the pace.
Avg: (Tempo_A + Tempo_B) / 2
Slow / Fast: min() / max()
06
Spread & Total
The spread is the absolute difference in projected scores. The over/under is the sum of both projected scores.
Spread = |Score_A − Score_B|
Total = Score_A + Score_B
